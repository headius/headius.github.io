--- layout: post title: The Ever-Evolving JVM date: '2007-07-15T12:16:00.000-07:00' author: headius tags: modified_time: '2011-01-25T21:44:32.672-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-4704664917418794835.post-6925440263676799958 blogger_orig_url: http://blog.headius.com/2007/07/ever-evolving-jvm.html --- John Rose, lead of the "invokedynamic" effort (Java Specification Request 292), has posted some exciting articles about the future of the JVM and a number of changes potentially for the next Java version. Among these is, of course, the dynamic invocation efforts, but these entries include information on non-local returns from closures, tail calls, and tuple support. I'm excited to have John as a co-worker and to be helping out the invokedynamic effort in my own small way by working on JRuby's compiler. John has also provided guidance on how to make a dynamic language fast on current JVMs, which has informed much of my compiler's design.
