This Q/A is based on statements I've seen in various news articles, blog posts, and on twitter.

What's happening to JRuby?

The core JRuby team members Tom Enebo, Charles Oliver Nutter, and Nick Sieger are moving from Sun Microsystems to Engine Yard to continue working on JRuby.

Why leave Sun?

All three of us really, really loved working at Sun. It was a dream job, and we appreciated the opportunity to work with some of the best engineers in the business. But with the Oracle buyout in progress, the future was totally opaque. We couldn't make any promises about JRuby's future. We couldn't plan our own future on the project. And perhaps worst of all, we had no idea whether the axe might fall and we'd be out of work. JRuby is heavily community driven, and being unable to assure the community about JRuby's future was very painful for us.

So as much as we loved working for Sun, we decided to make a move for the good of the project. Engine Yard reached out to us, offered to bring all three of us on board, and spelled out their plans to support cloud, hosting, and support options for JRuby.
