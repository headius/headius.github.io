--- layout: post title: Burnette and Monson Haefel on Android date: '2007-11-28T21:16:00.000-08:00' author: headius tags: modified_time: '2011-01-25T21:44:32.155-08:00' blogger_id: tag:blogger.com,1999:blog-4704664917418794835.post-520546322838590477 --- I've noticed a couple Android posts from Richard Monson-Haefel have been answered by a post from Ed Burnette. Interesting points on both sides.

First RMH asks "Is Android Fragmentation Inevitable?", largely because certain portions of the API are optional. Here, he may have a point. One of the strengths of Java ME was the profiles; you could write for a specific set of APIs in a given profile and know your app would always work on other devices that support that profile. But it's early days for Android; I'd expect there would be certain compatibility levels established as things move along. Then he goes on to describe "Why Microsoft Loves Google's Android".
